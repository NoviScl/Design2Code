Q: Do the Chambrer Wine Cellars have a compressor?

A: No, there is no compressor inside the wine cellars. They use thermal-electrics to cool the interior using a Peltier System.

Q: Do the Wine Cellars contain compressed gases like a regular refrigerator?

A: No, there are no compressed gases used in the cooling system of the wine cellars.

Q: Do the Chambrer Wine Cellars use a large amount of energy?

A: No, they use less energy than a 100 watt light-bulb.

Q: Why does the temperature display flash when I adjust the temperature using the (+) or (-) or (preset) buttons?

A: When using the (+) or (-) buttons, a flashing display means the cellar is cooling to the desired temperature. When using the (preset) button, a flashing display means the cellar is cooling to the desired preset temperature.

Back to top

Q: What does a non-blinking display mean?

A: A non-blinking display means the current temperature inside the wine cellar is being displayed.

Back to top

Q: Is it normal for the fan to be always running?

A: Yes, this is a normal function of the cooling system. The lower the temperature setting, the more the fan will run. Additionally, if the room is warmer than 24oC (75oF), it will run more often to maintain the interior temperature.

Q: The unit keeps on turning on and off every couple of minutes. Why?

A: There is nothing wrong with the wine cellar as this is a normal function of the cooling system. The wine cellar has a temperature tolerance of +/- 1 °C and the wine cellar is turning on and off to maintain the temperature within 1 °C degree of the set temperature.

Q: Can I recess my Chambrer Wine Cellar into a cupboard?

A: No, you cannot recess nor build in our wine cellars. All wine cellars require at least 3.5 cm (1.4 inches) of ventilation around all vents to function properly.

Q: What is the operating temperature of the Chambrer Wine Cellar?

A: The operating temperature range are from 7oC (45oF) to 20oC (68oF) because wine should not be stored outside this temperature range. Please note that the protection mechanism only works when used under the recommended ambient temperature conditions. The recommended ambient room temperature should be 24oC (75oF) or cooler for the unit to function properly.

Q: Do I have to clean my Chambrer Wine Cellar?

A: Yes. Below is a listing of general cleaning tips that should be taken when cleaning your Chambrer Wine Cellar.

Be cautious when using any type of cleaning solvents, some individuals may have allergic reactions to chemicals in cleaning solvents and some solvents can even damage the wine cellar. Try to always use water or a highly diluted solvent. It is best to use water or rubbing alcohol. Other solvents may be bad for the wine cellar.

Never spray or squirt any type of liquid onto your wine cellar. If a spray is needed, spray the liquid onto a cloth and then use the moistened cloth to rub down the wine cellar.

Q: Should I clean the outside fans of my Chambrer Wine Cellar?

A: Yes, because dirt, dust and hair can build up causing the cooling system to not function as efficiently.

Back to top

Q: How do you clean the outside fans of my Chambrer Wine Cellar?

A: ALWAYS UNPLUG THE UNIT FROM THE WALL PRIOR TO CLEANING THE FANS! An effective method to clean the fan is to use compressed air. Compressed air is pressurized air contained in a can with a very long nozzle. Ensure to follow the directions of the compressed air container to avoid damage or personal injury. Simply aim the air at the fans and blow away all of the dust and debris that has gathered. A vacuum cleaner can also be used at the same time to collect the loose dust particles.

Back to top

Q: How often should I clean the outside fans of my Chambrer Wine Cellar?

A: We recommend cleaning the external fans of the wine cellar every 4 months for efficient operation of the cooling system.
