3. Woodsia ilvensis (Linnaeus) R. Brown, Trans. Linn. Soc. London, Bot. 11: 173. 1813.

Rusty cliff fern, woodsie de l'île d'Elbe

Acrostichum ilvense Linnaeus, Sp. Pl. 2: 1071. 1753

Stems compact, erect to ascending, with abundant persistent petiole bases of ± equal length; scales uniformly brown, lanceolate. Leaves 4.5--25 × 1.2--3.5 cm.

Sporulating summer--early fall. Cliffs and rocky slopes; found on variety of substrates including serpentine; 0--1500 m; Greenland; Alta., B.C., Man., N.B., Nfld., N.W.T., N.S., Ont., Que., Sask., Yukon; Alaska, Conn., Ill., Iowa, Maine, Md., Mass., Mich., Minn., N.H., N.J., N.Y., N.C., Ohio, Pa., R.I., Vt., Va., W.Va., Wis.; n Eurasia.

Although generally separable by the characters given in the key, shade forms of Woodsia ilvensis with a reduced number of scales and hairs are occasionally misidentified as W . alpina .
