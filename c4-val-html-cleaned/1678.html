Three days to Sydney, part two

Published on 2013-02-28 · Travel

Deja vu . Goodbyes, a security and passport check, a bus ride aaaaand no turboprop airplane :) Wow, this time we might even fly over Slovenia.

Hour 0

Team Sydney, the three person cell of combined age of more than 150 years, prepared for take off. I was sitting next to an emergency exit which means I had 20cm of legroom more.

Frankfurt met us with cold and rain. We got out, drove on a train to another terminal and sat for a drink in front of our gate.

Hour 4, Qantas

Australian airline Qantas is the second oldest in the world, dating from 1920s. During boarding their Boeing 747 I noticed most of the staff was 40 or older and almost of them were men.

I was sitting between two people, but when the pilot said that there were many free seats and we could relocate after the seatbelt sign turned off, I became a man with a mission.

Hour 5

The taxi and the take-off took a while, but when the sign turned off, I was in a low start and ready. Fifteen seconds later I got myself a seat next to an aisle which allowed me to extend my legs.

Hour 9

I’m wide awake after only three hours. This is not good; we still have seven hours to go.

Just a note for future flights. Foam ear plugs can isolate four extra powerful jet engines, but a baby’s cry will cut through them like a hot knife through butter.

Hour 15

I slowly woke up when the staff turned the lights on. I found myself pushed out of my seat by a granny who occupied three seats already.

A smell of the second in-flight meal spread through the cabin and the granny woke up with a smile. At least someone had a good and uninterrupted sleep.

Hour 18, Singapore

I rushed through the gate and headed straight for the bathroom. I won’t go into any details, but beware of the toilets.

A fresh shirt, wet wipes and a toothbrush can be a resurrection on such long flights so be sure to pack them with you.

Two things caught my attention: free Internet, and kids in military uniforms and bulletproof vests, holding machine guns. Other than that, just an ordinary airport.

I went through the procedures and paperwork with Leo and Mary, and then helped them by translating on a security check. We boarded the plane again and were ready for the last leg of the trip.

Hour 20, Are we there yet?

I got the middle seat again, but this time the flight was full, so I was stuck. The fact that I wasn’t sleepy at all made it even worse.

Hour 28

I was so happy when I saw Sydney for the first time during descent. It marked the end of this too long voyage.

I took Team Sydney through the passport and customs control, and then to baggage claim. I reported some stuff they thought could be suspicious, so a dog had to sniff me and my baggage.

Leo’s family was waiting for us outside. They were so happy to see Team Sydney arrive safely and they thanked me through tears in a mix of Croatian and English.

Sydney, here I come .
