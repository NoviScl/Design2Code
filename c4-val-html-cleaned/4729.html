At last some contrary evidence:
It is possible to maintain a conviction so intense that one rejects conflicting evidence.  One of the most blood curdling examples is a book called The Witches’ Hammer by a couple of inquisitors Sprenger and Kramer.  The book was immediately rejected by the Catholic Church, but since there were a lot of witch trials and no other book to tell you how to do it, the wretched thing survived.  They say that the judge at the Salem witch trials had a copy on his desk.

What Sprenger and Kramer did when faced with what they thought was contrary evidence was to explain it away as a devilish plot to discredit them. There is no arguing against something like that.

Over the years I have found a lot of supporting evidence. I do not deliberately suppress what seems to be in conflict with me, but I find so little of it that I rarely am put to the test.

In order for me to have a problem with that, I must satirize my own position that marriages between unrelated people produce fewer children and that this accumulates over generations. Let me phrase it, “Outbreeding is the root of all evil.”  That’s not what I think, but it’s close enough for present purposes.

Marriages are in decline. Right.

The divorce rate is high and rising. Right.

There aren’t enough babies for us to survive. Right.

But look a little closer and a crack appears. As it turns out, divorce and out of wedlock children happen more among the poor than among the rich.

Oops.

It might be possible to do some hand waving and reconcile the facts. But without some hard data, such hand waving would remain unconvincing.
