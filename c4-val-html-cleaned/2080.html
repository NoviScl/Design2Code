Imaging analysis to better understand skin features in allergic contact dermatitis (ACD)

This is a single site study to gain better understanding of the skin changes in allergic contact dermatitis (ACD), a skin condition caused by an allergic reaction to compounds in contact with the skin. Subjects will be patients who present to Banner University Medical Center (BUMC) dermatology clinic for routine patch testing to identify potential compound that may be causing their pre-exiting rash. Any positive skin reactions found on patch testing reading examination will be photographed using a multimodal digital camera (MDC) that can capture high resolution skin surface images and information beneath the surface.

Basic eligibility criteria:
Please contact the study coordinator for additional eligibility information.

Inclusion criteria:
• At least 18 years of age.
• Found to have positive skin reaction(s) to found on routine patch testing
• Ability to understand and the willingness to sign a written consent document in English.

Primary disease category: Dermatology

Projected enrollment dates: December 2016 to December 2018

Official study title: Imaging analysis to better understand skin features in allergic contact dermatitis (ACD)
