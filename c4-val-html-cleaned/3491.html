Common Name: Sweet Joe-Pye Weed

Attracts:  Bees, Butterflies

Use:  Garden, Naturalizing

Light:  Full Sun to Full Shade

Hardiness Zone:  4 to 9  USDA Zone Map

Soils:  Moist 

Flower Time:  Mid-Juy to Late September

Flower Color:  Mauve Pink

Height: 5' - 7'

Notes:  Eutrochium purpureum (Sweet Joe-Pye Weed) is nice in open woodlands and naturalizing areas.  Great value to bees and butterflies.  More tolerant of drier soils than Eutrochium maculatum.  Noted as deer-resistant by UVM.

Native to the Northeast (except ME, ﻿RARE IN VT), Mid-Atlantic, Southeast and Midwest, US:   Biota of North America Program (BONAP) – North American Plant Atlas (NAPA).
