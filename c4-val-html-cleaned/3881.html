About...
Virginia Beach is the most populated city in Virginia, with a population of nearly half a million. This seaside city attracts millions of dollars in tourist revenue as visitors flock to the extensive beaches, restaurants, and entertainment establishments. Home to a massive aquarium, an outdoor amphitheater that hosts several annual festivals, and numerous buildings on the National Register of Historic Places, Virginia Beach is full of culture and history.

The United States military is a large employer in the city. The Navy’s Naval Air Station Oceana is the largest employer. The Navy also hosts the Training Support Center Hampton Roads, the Army’s Fort Story is located in the city’s boundaries.

Farms around the city offer fresh produce at local farmer’s markets. The agricultural community also contributes millions of dollars to the city’s economy with diverse crops that include wheat, dairy, and horse breeding.

A diverse economy has kept this city strong and growing. New residents will enjoy the myriad tourist activities, cultural events including the Neptune Festival and American Music Festival, and the area’s colonial history. With so many employment fields to choose from, it’s surprising that more people are not moving to Virginia Beach.
