BRC for sure has the ability to maintain 1.7c dividend, but they have skipped the special dividend which usually in 1st half of the year, so I guess total dividend for the year will be lower. Nonetheless, the company has paid a total of 7.9c of dividend for the last 8 years, which is close of 6% yield annually. I thought this is a good return for a company like BRC who is ridding on industry trend and with low gearing. If the company can maintain 1.7c dividend for the year, it yields more than 10% and would be a strong catalyst to push up the share price. Another comfort level is that current price is more than 15% cheaper than the bond holders conversion price, I think the MOS is sufficient already.
