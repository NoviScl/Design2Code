Practice

The best way to improve and learn is to practice. And then practice more.

Review

The Resources page provides booklets, videos, and DVDs that can be reviewed at home. These materials allow students to review what we practice in class at home and then have the teacher correct in the classroom.

Warm-up

Always remember to stretch -- start slow and easy and work your way to bigger stretches and harder moves. Just like we do in class.

  • Waving and swinging arms
  • Slowly rotating and stretching kneck
  • touching toes (standing and sitting)
  • rotating and bending knees and ankles
  • Spider-walk down leg to hold foot and extend leg
  • Crescents (in/out, single/double)
  • Situps, pushups
  • Fu jacks

Basics

Remember to continually practice your basics -- even when you progress in belts. The basics are the foundation of everything so keep practicing them.

  • Punches (left/right, high/med/low)
  • Block combo
  • Kick combo
  • Stance combo
  • 4 elbow drill

Forms

Practice your forms over and over, just like in class. The forms you practice depend on your level, but the basic forms include the following:

  • Basic T (white --> low yellow)
  • Basic One (low yellow --> high yellow)
  • Basic Sword (low yellow --> high yellow)
  • Naihanshi Shodan (high yellow --> low orange)
  • Basic Two (high yellow --> low orange)
  • Basic Tonfa (high yellow --> low orange)
  • Basic Kama (high yellow --> low orange)
  • 12 Angles of Attack (high yellow --> low orange)
  • Basic Staff (high yellow --> low orange)
