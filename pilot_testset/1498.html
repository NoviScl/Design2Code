Organic Garden

Organic Garden

We have organic gardens around drive way, office and next to the pasture.

We use compost and natural fertilizers as our plant foods, and we grow natural pest repellants (scented flowers and herbs) around fruits and vegetables to minimize the damages from bugs and birds, and make a spray mixture of herbs and water to spray on the plants as another natural pest repellent. However, we wouldn’t mind to share some of our fruits and vegetables with birds, butterflies, bees and cute baby rabbits.

Our growing seasons start in late April through early October.  Here are some of the vegetables and herbs you can find in our gardens:

We built a greenhouse in February, 2018, so might have longer growing seasons. We use this to allow seeds to be started during cold weather months, and to provide a safe space for tropical plants in winter.

Vegetables

Asparagus – We grow Jersey Giant and Sweet Purple. They can be harvested from late April to June.

Corn – We grow Black and Gold sweet corns. The best harvest time is from Late June to July.

Cucumber – They can be harvested from June to August. They are great for salad.

Greens – We grow all kinds of salad greens and cabbages. They are our energy booster.

Peas and Beans – peas start really early in the season, they don’t like heat much, so they will die down when summer starts. It starts from April and last till late May.

Pepper – We like the color of all sweet peppers, so we plant one of each every year. Of course, we will add some hot peppers to spice it up.

Potato – we mainly grow Purple Peruvian and Yukon Gold because they are healthier and we think they taste better than Idaho potato. We try some new verities from time to time, too.

Summer Squash and Zucchini – They grow fast but have a short season, they can be harvested from June to July. Add some olive oil/butter, salt and pepper, they are wonderful on the grill.

Sweet Potato – We grow Japanese Yam and Purple sweet potatoes. They have the taste of sweet potatoes, but texture of potato.

Tomato –we have seven to eight kinds of tomato every year, it starts to ripe from late May to early September. They are great for salad or soup.

Turnips and Carrots – Turnips are pretty easy to grow. They can be harvested from April to June and from September to October.

Herbs

Basil – Basil is a great companion plant with Tomato. They grow well together and they taste great together.

Bee Balm – They are available from late April to early October. Some people like to use the leaves to make tea, we like them for the pretty flowers.

Chives and Garlic Chives – Chives are for all seasons, they grow from very cold winter and die down some during hot summer. Their leaves are great addition for soup and salad.

Garlic – We grow a few kinds of garlic plants around roses, fruits and greens. Some customers really like our garlic, especially the native mountain garlic and the elephant garlic.

German Chamomile – The flowers are great for tea or cosmetic. They bloom from May to July.

Lavender – They are almost available for all seasons. Some people use them in tea, we only use them to make soaps or dry flowers.

Lemon Balm – They are available from late April to early October. They are great to be added in tea.

Mint and Pepper Mint – They are available from late April to early October.  Spear Mint is great to be added to ice tea or water, Pepper Mint tea is wonderful to drink after a heavy meal.

Onion – We grow Siskiyou Sweet Onion and Red Onion. We also have the Egypian Walking Onion so you can harvest them easier (on top of plants).

Rosemary – They are almost available for all seasons. They are great with potato or chicken.

Sage - They are almost available for all seasons.   They are great with chicken or turkey.

Thyme - We use Thyme as ground cover around the gardens, they are also a great addition to many of my home cooking receipes.

We also grow Bay Leave, Lemon, and Lemon grass around the office porch.  They are not winter hardy, so we have to put them indoor for the winter season.

Fruits

Generally, Blue Berries, Straw Berries, Gooseberries and Blackberries are ready between May and June. Melons, Jojobe are ready between August and September.

If the weather permitted (sometime, we have late frost that kill most of the flowers or young fruits), we will have Plums, Apples and Pears available starting from late July to Mid October.  

We also have Persimmons, Musadines, Mayhow fruits, Goji Berries, Green, Peach and Nectarine.

Flowers

We like to grow flowers around vegetables to attract lady bugs, bees, butterflies and birds. Our favorite ones are Cleome, Cosmos, Four O’Clock, Dahlia, Holly Hock, Lamb’s Ear, Marigold, Sun flowers, Mexican Sun flowers, and Zinnia.
