FAUN SLT 50-2

Notes: Though it was supposed to be replaced by the FS 42.75/42, the SLT 50-2 continues to serve on with the German Army in a supplemental role as a tank transporter, and in towing other types of semi-trailers. Also known to the Germans as the Elefant, the SLT 50-2 was originally designed starting in 1965 to tow the abortive MBT-70 tank, but was kept when that project was cancelled for other duties.

The cab of the SLT 50-2 is of the forward control tipper type, constructed of steel and glass fiber composites. The suspension is 8x8 and steerable on the front two axles.

IVECO Magirus 330-40 ANWTM

Notes: The 330-40 ANWTM is a semi-tractor based upon the Magirus line of trucks produced by IVECO. Formerly known as the Magirus 400M33AS, this truck is a 6x6 vehicle with a heavy-duty cross-country suspension.

The 330-32 ANWTM is the predecessor of the 330-40 ANWTM; in physical appearance, it is virtually identical to the 330-40 ANWTM, but the 330-32 ANWTM uses a non-turbocharged version of the engine used in the newer vehicle. The 330-32 ANWTM cannot use the special tires for soft terrain that the 330-40 ANWTM can use.

Mercedes-Benz 2636 AS

Notes: This semi-tractor’s primary use is with heavy tank transporter trailers, though other types of semi-trailers may be towed by this truck. The cab is of the forward-control tipper type, with a heavy bumper up front and room for three in the sleeper cab.

The 3850 AS is an improved version of the 2636 AS. It has a longer wheelbase, and uses a more powerful version of the same engine as the 2636 AS.

The 4050 A is an 8x8 version of 3850 AS, and shares several components with that model. The main differences between the two trucks is the 4050 As higher fifth wheel loading and rear axles’ suspensions, allowing it to tow heavier loads.
